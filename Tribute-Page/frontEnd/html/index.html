About Sarah Kay

Sarah Kay is an American poet, educator, and the co-founder of Project V.O.I.C.E., an organization that uses spoken word to educate and empower students and teachers worldwide.

Kay is best known for her spoken word poetry, but is also the author of four bestselling books, a graduate of Brown University's Masters of the Art of Teaching Secondary English Program, and a three-time TED Talk presenter. She has preformed in over 30 countires in venues such as Carnegie Hall, the United Nations, the Tribeca Film Festival, Lincoln Center Out of Doors, the Nantucket Project, the Apollo Theater, the Royal Danish Theatre, and more.

Kay an education advocate has touched millions with her work. Her spoken word preformances amass millions of views on YouTube and she continues to inspire with her work and advocacy.

"I tell them, "Listen. Listen to one another like you know you are scholars. Artists. Scientists. Athletes. Musicians. Like you know you will be the ones to shape this world. Show me how many colors you know how to draw with. Show me how proud you are of what you have learned. And I promise I will do the same."

-Sarah Kay, Mrs. Ribeiro
